Microbial function influences plant traits

Nov 18, 2022 microbial ecology, plant traits, microbial function

Aquatic ecosystems are comprised of complex and diverse communities of animals, plants, invertebrates, and microbes, all interacting with each other and their environment. A challenge of working with complex communities is they are often large and vary across different scales. To test community-level hypotheses in a tractable system, we investigated the effect of divergent microbial community function on host health using the purple pitcher plant, Sarracenia purpurea, as a model system for plant-microbiome interactions. Microbial communities play important roles in mediating the health and stability of ecosystems, where they perform vital functions like cycling nutrients through biosynthesis and decomposition.
